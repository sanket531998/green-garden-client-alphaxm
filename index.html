What is it?

The word "vermi" is Latin for worm and "compost" stands for the manure. The word manure itself signifies that it is Organic.

How is it made?

It is the practice of using worms to break down organic material into a mix of worm castings(worm manure).

Frequently Asked Questions

Vermicomposting is a popular alternative to composting and has several benefits for home gardeners. Because the worms actually eat vegetable and fruit scraps, the scraps are degraded faster than in typical composting systems. Vermicomposting can also be done indoors throughout the winter, whereas typical composting slows or stops during the winter months.

  • What is vermicompost?

    Decomposed mixture of vegetales, food waste, bedding materials and vermicast (worm castings, worm humus, worm manure, or worm faeces). Decomposition is carried out by using worms like earthworms, white worms and red wigglers.

  • Why should we use vermicompost?

    Diverting waste from landfills is excellent, and vermicomposting also produces nutrient-rich castings for your garden.

    -Compared to traditional composting less space is needed.

    -Less strength is needed.

    -Faster production of compost.

    -Red wiggler worms eat half their weight in food waste daily.

    -Improves soil properties such aeration, improves water holding capacity, enriches soil with micro-organisms.

    -It helps in root and plant growth.

    -Reduces greenhouse gas emissions such as methane and nitric oxide.

  • Why should you use our product?

    Our product is 100% virgin and organic, it will boost your plant growth, stimulate the defence mechanisim and improve the immune system of your plant.

  • Where can we use vermicompost ?

    Agriculture, small scale organic farming, kitchen gardening, container gardening and indoor gardening.

  • What are the properties of vermicompost?

    It contains Earthy smell, organic nutrients, soil conditioner and minerals like zinc, copper, sulphur. Molybdenum, manganese, magnesium, calcium, boron and iron.
